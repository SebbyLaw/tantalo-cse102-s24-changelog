General Instructions

Written homework assignments will be submitted and reviewed via CrowdGrader.  Begin by creating an account on CrowdGrader with user name identical to your CruzID@ucsc.edu email address.  For each homework assignment, perform the following steps.
  • Work all problems carefully and neatly.  You may write your solutions by hand, or you may use a text editor.  CrowdGrader includes an editor supporting mathematical notation.  Note that neatness and readability will count towards your grade.  Do not include your name or identifying information on your homework paper. We will identify you through your CrowdGrader account name.

  • Scan your paper as a pdf file.  There are many free scanner apps available for both iPhone and Android.  Some recommended scanners are:

    Evernote Scannable for iOS devices, and
    Genius Scan for Android devices.

    If you use the CrowdGrader editor, no scan will be necessary.

  • The submission period will last 2 days (Monday, Tuesday), and the assignment will be visible in your CrowdGrader account during that time.  Upload your pdf before the deadline by following the submission link.

  • The review period will begin after solutions are posted, and will last 5 days (Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday).  CrowdGrader will present you with the papers of 5 randomly selected students.  Use the posted solutions, together with the CrowdGrader rubric, to evaluate these papers.  The rubric will consist of three catagories: neatness, completeness and correctness.

  • You will receive two grades, one based on your homework submission (your submission grade), and the other based on your accuracy as a reviewer (your review grade).  These grades will be combined (80% submission grade + 20% review grade) to give your crowdgrade, which will be your score on the assignment.

  • If you have any problems with the above procedures, or if you wish to appeal a homework grade, contact Teaching Assistant Saeed Kargar (skargar@ucsc.edu)
